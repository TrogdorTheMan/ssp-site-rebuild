More Seafair Traditions

The customs that connect Seafair's past to its present

Seafair Skipper Pins

Collectible annual pins featuring ship wheels and hydroplane imagery have been produced since 1950. Originally sold for $1.00, they've become cherished keepsakes of each year's festival.

Seafair Skipper Pins, 1950 and 1956

Seafair Royalty

Since 2000, one King Neptune and one Queen Alcyone are selected annually based on their philanthropic contributions to the Puget Sound region and their ability to inspire their communities.

Queen Alcyone and King Neptune

"Seafair royalty are chosen not for pageantry alone, but for the mark they've already made on the community."

Miss Seafair

Selected through the Seafair Foundation's Scholarship Program, Miss Seafair is evaluated on academic achievement, public speaking, and community service. In 2023, Savannah Parker made history as the first African American woman crowned in the program's history.

Miss Seafair 2023 — Savannah Parker

Seafair Pirates

Seattle's high-humored heist since 1949. Community servants, entertainers, and keepers of Seafair tradition.
